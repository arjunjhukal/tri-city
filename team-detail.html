Deependra Gauchan

Deependra Gauchan is a pioneering filmmaker with a career spanning nearly four decades, marked by over 20 films, including documentaries, short films, and feature films across South Asia, Southeast Asia, and Africa. He is celebrated for his commitment to social change, exemplified in projects like *UJELI* (1992) and *GUHAAR!* (1997), where he trained rural women in Nepal to create films. A co-founder of Nepal's first film and television training institute, AA-VAS, Gauchan has been instrumental in nurturing new talent. His international work includes leading significant communications projects for BBC Media Action in countries like Cambodia and Ethiopia. Currently based in Cambodia, Gauchan serves as an Adviser to the Department of Cinema and Cultural Diffusion and mentors young filmmakers. As head of Tri-City Pictures, he is dedicated to bringing Nepalese and Cambodian cinema to the global stage, collaborating with industry leaders to produce innovative films.
